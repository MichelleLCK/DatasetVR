Sample Data 1: Cultured Cancer Cell Lines

We imaged 4 types of cancer cell lines (i.e. HL60 of human leukemia cell line, H1975 and H2170 of human lung cancer cell lines, MB231 of human breast cancer cell lines), on 3 different days. The cells were captured under two label-free contrast (i.e. BF, QPI), forming 3 batches of cells with a total of ~2,000,000 cells in total.

Sample Data 2: Lung Cancer Cell Lines

We imaged 7 types of lung cancer cell lines (H358, H1975, HCC827, H520, H2170, H526 and H69) to facilitate lung cancer analyses. We collected the data on 7 different days, contributing to 3 batches of ~120,000 cells per cell line (i.e. >2,000,000 single-cell images in total, each consists of two label-free contrasts: BF and QPI). These 7 types of lung cancer cell lines could simply be categorized in 3 major lung cancer types, which is lung squamous cell carcinoma (H520, H2170), adenocarcinoma (H358, H1975, HCC827) and small cell carcinoma (H69, H526).

Sample Data 3: Drug Treatment Response of H2170 (Gemcitabine)

H2170 were treated with 3 types of drugs, each of them based on a different mechanism of action (MoA) (i.e. Docetaxel as microtubule stabilizing agent, Afatinib as tyrosine kinase inhibitor in targeted therapy and Gemcitabine as antimetabolite). H2170 were cultured under 5 concentration levels and under dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO) as negative control for 24 hours. The experiment was conducted on 6 days and cells were imaged using multi-ATOM for acquiring single-cell BF and QPI images, eventually forming 2 batches of data for each drug and collected a total of ~3,000,000 cell images.
